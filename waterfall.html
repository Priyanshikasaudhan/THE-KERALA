Waterfalls In Kerala

Kerala is the land of mesmerizing natural beauty that is best discovered with a Kerala travel package. Beyond the jungles and the tea plantations, you have another resplendent phenomenon to look forward to when visiting the state – waterfalls in Kerala that attrac t thousands of sightseers every year. Fascinating, right?! Here’s a list of the top 10 waterfalls in Kerala that are also among the best tourist places to visit in Kerala: –

  • Athirappilly Waterfalls

Athirappilly Waterfalls originates from the Western Ghats and is located at the beginning of Sholayar Ranges. Falling from a height of 80 feet, this is the biggest waterfall in Kerala. Extending the beauty of this cascade is the lush vegetation of Vazhachal Forest which is an abode for diverse wildlife such as elephants, bison, tigers, leopards, hornbills and sambar deer, along with some of the endemic species of flora. Being the biggest waterfall of Kerala, Athirappilly Waterfalls is regarded as the Niagra Falls of India! No wonder it is one of the most famous waterfalls in the state.

  • Vazhachal Waterfalls

Take a break from the hustle-bustle around Athirappilly Waterfalls. Travel through the serenity of Vazhachal Forest to reach Vazhachal Falls. This one is a short drive away from Athirappilly Waterfalls and among the more secluded ones. A quiet and composed atmosphere surrounds this waterfall in Kerala where the stream gushes with such velocity that you can hear it on your way to Vazhachal Falls.

  • Palaruvi Waterfalls

Palaruvi means milk in Malayalam. Palaruvi gets its name from the fact that the water flowing here appears to the spectators as a stream of milk flowing through the woods. One of the most famous waterfalls in Kerala, Palaruvi is believed to have Ayurvedic healing properties for skin problems. The place is frequented as a picnic spot by the locals and tourists alike You have to trek through the dense forests and cross streams of water to reach this waterfall, which adds a hint of adventure to the experience.

  • Meenmutty Waterfalls

Meenmutty Waterfalls has something for both adventure lovers as well as nature lovers. A 2 km long trek through the dense forest and a rocky terrain will take you to Meenmutty Waterfalls. The moment you reach there, you are left awestruck by the sight of a foamy stream of water falling from the height of around 1000 feet, over three separate levels and crashing into a large pool of water. Owing to its magnitude, Meenmutty Falls is counted among the largest waterfalls of Kerala.

  • Thommankuttu Waterfalls

Adventure aficionados cannot miss on visiting Thommankuttu Waterfalls. Trekking 12 kms up the hills and through the woods to reach this waterfall is a great challenge in itself. Cascading down 7 tiers, it offers majestic views to those who have a knack of capturing the details. Your visit to this waterfall in Kerala can be made more interesting with activities like fishing and bird-watching. So, after you are done trekking, have some relaxing time at the shores of Thommankuttu.
