Kerala Backwaters-

We have all heard of the greenery, backwaters and boathouses of Kerala. But what exactly are backwaters and how can one get to enjoy the best backwaters in Kerala? Backwaters are parts of the river where there are little to no currents and Kerala backwaters are a famous tourism product. Let’s explore the 10 best backwaters in Kerala.

Alleppey Backwaters

Located in Alleppey are the backwaters widely
known for famous the boat race held in August.
This is one of the most striking backwaters as the
picturesque here will leave you speechless
especially during the serene sunsets.To make the
best of it; you can either
opt for a modest houseboat or take a local
budget-friendly ferry. This is also
a great place for those who enjoy bird watching
as you can spot some of the most
exotic and rare kind of birds in this region.

Thiruvananthapuram Backwaters

Have you heard of the traditional houseboats “Kettuvalloms’?
Thiruvanathapuram is the rarest
Kerala backwaters where you will
get the opportunity to experience cruising
in one of these. Also,
famous for the canoe rides; this backwater is where
the two rivers
Killi and Karamana. When in Kerala; you
must try the ride at least once!

Kozhikode Backwaters

Kozhikode is the best Kerala backwaters when it
comes to clean, unspoiled backwaters in Kerala.
The beach has a lighthouse and two piers which
give you the view and the serenity both. On your
tour, you will also get a chance to halt at the
Pazhassirajah Museum that holds a rare collection
of coins in silver and gold that were used by Vasco
Di Gama and the Britishers. If you are looking to
have the perfect houseboat cruising experience
then you should try out Kerala backwaters at

Thrissur Backwaters

If you are looking for the best backwaters in Kerala
and have already been to the Alleppey backwaters,
then Thrissur Backwaters are a must-visit site.
Giving a tough competition to Alleppey you can
also go for motorboats here if you are bored
of houseboats or want to enjoy the Kerala
backwaters in a different manner.
Thrissur will give you a peek into both
the modern as well as the local Kerala
living. Yet another alternative is to catch
a shikara to go cruising around Chettuva Lake,
Kanoli Kanal and Tripayar.

Kuttanad Backwaters

Flowing between the hills and the sea; Kuttanad ,
Backwaters are in perfect harmony with a fleet of
ducks quacking their way through the waters.
On one side you will get to see the greenery,
and on the other, you can spot the local,
rural Kerala village life. You can catch a
sight of the paddy fields and beauty of the
surroundings while touring your way
through the Kuttanad backwaters.
Other tourist attractions on the route
are Champakulam and Chengannur.
